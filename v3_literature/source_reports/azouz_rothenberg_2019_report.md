## 2. Summary

This is a comprehensive review article covering the immunologic, genetic, environmental, and microbiome-related mechanisms of gastrointestinal allergic disorders. The review places EoE within the broader spectrum of food antigen–driven allergic diseases and discusses the epithelial barrier impairment pathway (including SPINK7 loss, DSG1 reduction, and CAPN14 induction). It references the SPINK7-KLK5 pathway established in S-02 and S-03 but does not report original experimental data. The review summarizes genetic associations (TSLP, CAPN14, IL-13, SERPINB), monogenic disorders (Netherton syndrome/SPINK5, SAM syndrome/DSG1), and emerging therapies (anti-IL-13, anti-TSLP, A1AT replacement, OIT).

## 3. Identified Features

### Features Not Found / Not Applicable

This source is a narrative review and does not contain original quantitative experimental values. It references primary studies (S-02, S-03) for SPINK7-KLK5 data. Key contextual points relevant to our pipeline:

- SPINK7 inhibits uPA and controls protease activity in the esophagus (referencing S-02)
- SPINK5 loss-of-function (Netherton syndrome) demonstrates clinical consequences of protease-inhibitor imbalance
- TSLP × PLAU genetic epistasis supports the protease pathway (referencing S-02)
- Figure 1 provides a pathway diagram positioning SPINK7 as the upstream checkpoint

No $K_i$, $K_d$, $\Delta G$, $k_{\text{cat}}$, or structural measurements are reported.

## 4. Overall Assessment

Review article providing disease/pathway context. No original quantitative data for pipeline benchmarking. Value is exclusively contextual — positions the SPINK7-KLK5 interaction within the broader GI allergic disease framework.
