## 1. Bibliographic Information

**Title:** Mutations in SPINK5, encoding a serine protease inhibitor, cause Netherton syndrome

**Authors:** Stéphane Chavanas, Christine Bodemer, Ariane Rochat, Dominique Hamel-Teillac, Mohsin Ali, Alan D. Irvine, Jean-Louis Bonafé, John Wilkinson, Alain Taïeb, Yann Barrandon, John I. Harper, Yves de Prost, Alain Hovnanian

**Journal:** *Nature Genetics*, 25, 141–142, 2000

---

## 2. Summary

Landmark brief communication identifying loss-of-function mutations in SPINK5 as the cause of Netherton syndrome. Reports 11 different mutations in 13 NS families. Establishes the LEKTI protein architecture (1,064 residues, 15 kazal-type domains, 33 exons). No biochemical or biophysical measurements reported.

---

## 3. Identified Features

### Feature 1: SPINK5/LEKTI Architecture

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| SPINK5 gene | 33 exons, 32 introns, ~61 kb |
| LEKTI protein | 1,064 amino acids, 15 kazal-type inhibitor domains |
| mRNA transcript | 3.7 kb |

### Feature 2: Disease-Causing Mutations

11 mutations identified: 1 nonsense (R790X), 4 insertions, 2 deletions, 4 splice-site mutations. Most predict premature termination → mRNA instability.

---

## 4. Overall Assessment

**Usefulness for Benchmarking: Low**

Foundational genetic discovery paper linking SPINK5 to Netherton syndrome. Provides essential biological context and LEKTI domain architecture but no quantitative biophysical data for pipeline benchmarking.
